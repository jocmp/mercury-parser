    India hosts Iran's Khamenei's confidant to convey position on Kashmir and counter criticism

    Synopsis

    India hosted Abdol Hossein Khosrow Panah, an advisor to Iran's supreme leader, to convey its stance on Kashmir. This visit, part of the Indian Council for Cultural Relations programme, aims to address concerns raised by Iranian leadership about India's treatment of Muslims.

    India hosted the secretary of Iran's Supreme Council for Cultural Revolution, Abdol Hossein Khosrow Panah, for a week-long programme last week, aiming to convey among other things its position on the Kashmir issue to Iran's supreme leader in the backdrop of views expressed by the Iranian leadership in this regard over the past year.

    Panah is close to Iran's supreme leader Ali Khamenei, whose views on critical global issues, including Kashmir, define the country's stance, according to people familiar with the matter.

    Last year, Khamenei had criticised India over the condition of Muslims in the country. India wanted to reach out to the supreme leader through one of his confidants, said one of the persons cited earlier.

    Panah was hosted under the Indian Council for Cultural Relations distinguished guest programme and his schedule included visits to the Taj Mahal and Rajghat.

    In September last year, the Indian government had deplored the comments made by Khamenei.
